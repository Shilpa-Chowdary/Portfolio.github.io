An Intuitive Projective Model with Probabilities of PSO+GA on Visegrad features with Multi domain Applicative Fraud Detections.


Introduction

In the realm of financial transactions, effective fraud detection is crucial for maintaining trust and security. Traditional methods often fall short against evolving fraudulent tactics. Our proposed system harnesses AI, including machine learning and deep learning, to analyze large transactional datasets with high efficiency and accuracy. Combining traditional algorithms with metaheuristic techniques like Genetic Algorithms and Particle Swarm Optimization, our project aims to detect fraud effectively and address dataset imbalance for fair and accurate classification. Our goal is to achieve prediction and classification accuracy surpassing 90%.

Objectives

Improve Detection Accuracy
Integrate AI models and metaheuristic techniques with traditional machine learning to achieve over 90% accuracy in detecting fraud in financial transactions for Visegrad Group listed companies.
Address Imbalanced Datasets
Develop a model to handle imbalanced financial datasets by implementing strategies within the AI model to mitigate biases and effectively identify and classify potential fraud instances, ensuring fairness and accuracy in classification.
Enhance Transparency and Interpretability
Integrate LIME and SHAP techniques to enhance transparency and interpretability in the AI model's decision-making process, fostering trust among users and enabling stakeholders to grasp the reasoning behind fraud classifications.

Representing the overall comparison of the proposed method accuracies with the existing algorithms

The comparison of existing algorithms with our proposed models shows significant advancements in predictive accuracy. Existing methods like AdaBoost, Gradient Boosting, and XG-Boost achieve accuracies ranging from 37.78% to 55.56%, while Random Forest and KNN reach 54.44% and 40.00% respectively.

In contrast, our models GA+ENSEMBLE and PSO+ENSEMBLE achieve remarkable validation accuracies of 97.8% and 98.6%. Interpolation Dense also demonstrates a notable enhancement with 94.76% accuracy. LIME and SHAP further achieve accuracies of 0.99 and 1.00 respectively. These results highlight our models' significant improvement in predictive accuracy compared to existing methods, showcasing their effectiveness in fraud detection within financial transactions.

In conclusion, our approach integrates Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) and Genetic Algorithms (GA) to enhance fraud detection accuracy in the Visegrad Group's financial transactions.
